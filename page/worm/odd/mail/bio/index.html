    On the internet, I did some research on Pete Martin. They say you can find everything there. So, I went to several search engines and typed in his name. I found many interesting things about Pete, some of which you might not have known.
    It seems Pete started off with a bike and a mission.
    Unfortunately, the only myth was Pete Martin himself. However, like most children of the cloth with a sore bum, he snapped and turned to a life of crime. Capitalizing on his knowledge of the trade, Pete started pushing bikes on the black market. Keith, his dealer and friend, wasn't too bright. As seen by this posting on a public bulletin board.
    Rich from his profiteering and desperate to shake his "good boy" image, Pete decided to crash the scene of hard drugs and easy women. Unfortunately, the closest he got was a case of Pig's Eye and a chick named Steph. Enclosed are some photos from a rally, near Todd's place, just out side of Mankato. As you can plainly see, by 3:00 Steph was feeling saucey and Jeremy was treats. Don't worry Jer, Officer Lorin will give you a warm place to sleep it off.
    After paying Bishop's bar tab and bail, most of Pete's wealth had vanished. Peter wanting a better life for him and Steph, tried to sober up and go legit. With the few dollars he had left, he decided to go into business with his, well...we'll say, good intentioned "Cousin Drew".
    Feeling that his entrepreneurial enterprise might be going down the crapper, Pete looked for other options. While reading "Savage Love" in the City Pages, he found an article in the back that said "College degree for $20 bucks!" So, he placed a phone call and after only 2 minutes he decided to do it. Reaching deep into Steph's savings Peter went for the bonus pack, which cost $30. Two weeks later, he received an envelope in the mail. The contents of which included a "realistic" diploma and a list of secondary credentials. Our lad would forever be known as Dr. Peter Martin. It looks as though, he even wrote a book.
    Armed with his new degree he went looking for a job. What Pete didn't realize is that his "Dr". designation didn't mean "Medical Doctor". Woefully, he applied and was turned down by everything from the Mayo to the Betty Ford Clinic. Although, they said they would have a place for him to stay any time he needed. In one promising interview, he was asked about his book "Sounds and Society". Of which, the lady interviewer read and throughly enjoyed. Pete mistakenly described the book as the sounds people make when they're "gettin' it on". This sounded like a very plausible subject matter based on the book's title. Moments later, he was chased out by armed security guards.

    The medical community soon caught wind of Pete's interview blunder. As a result, the the Betty Ford clinic cordially extended a second invitation for him to stay "the weekend". Pete, who occasional had a knack for the obvious, sensed this town might not be safe for him anymore. Accordingly, he started to look for out of state employment. Just when he thought he was out of luck, our boy found a position in the state of Washington. I guess they don't know what the "Dr." means either.
    A few short weeks later, a big story broke. Boy found dead at local college. Suddenly, Pete found himself in the dead center of it all.
    What you just read was an edited version of Pete's original professional opinion. Summarized, it declares that the man died of "natural causes". At random, the state's Chief Medical Officer reviews cases for quality assurance reasons. Unfortunately for Peter, this case was selected. In review, "the Chief" changed the ruling to homicide and Pete's performance was in question.

    As it turned out, the morphine found in the body was planted there by Pete's friend, Jefe. It was all a prank. I know what you are thinking, this doesn't sound that funny. But you have to remeber, Jefe has his own "brand of humor".

    The actual cause of death was concluded to be the knife found in the center of the boy's back. Dr. Pete originally documented this as "only a flesh wound". Shortly there after, a correction was published and Pete was forced to resign. As for Jefe, he was found to be clinically stupid and would remain in the care of professional. (We still love you man!)
    The gutless wonder decided he couldn't look Steph in those puppy dog eyes and tell her what happened. In a haze of sorrow, Pete drove out of town heading south. Eventually, he found himself at a cowboy bar in Waco, Texas. At approximitly 9:20 on a Sunday, a handsome man in chaps, asked this sorry son of a b*tch for a light. Unexpectedly, it was Peter's oldest and dearest compadre Jeremy.

    Immediatly, Bishop could tell something was wrong. In a moment of compassion, Jeremy asked "What the hell is worng with you?" Pete knew if he was going to shake this depression he needed admit what happened in the past. He also knew if he could share his feelings, with anyone, it would be Bishop. He delicatley began to tell his story. Quickly, Jeremy's emotions started to react to Pete's heart wrenching story. Tears welled, dripped, then poured across his checks. By the end of the story he was a wreck. His gut ached with pain. His voice all but gone. Jeremy had laughed to hard, occasionally uttering the words "What a dumb ass!" After picking himself up off the ground, Bishop was able to gain his composure. Always willing to help a brother out, Jeremey offered to let Peter stay in the basement of the bar. Did I forget to mention, after Waco's little run in with the Feds, Bishop bought everthing and revitilzed the town? Now you know!

    Pete would spend the next few years there, secluded, piliferating the internet with made up personalities, stories and accomplishments. Trying, in vain, to build an identity to reclaim what he had lost long ago.

    Exhibit: A B C F E F G H I
    Pete spent his "final days" looking at his hand having imaginary conversations with people who were long since dead. He was only 32, though he looked considerably older. Worry and endless unsettling despair made him weak and feeble. Deep lines were etched into his face each time he knowingly scripted another untruth about himself in cyberspace. Worst of all, the phrase "gay is lie... gay is lie" contiunally echoed in his head.
    It wasn't Pete's fault his life turned out this way. He was always trying to live up to his brother. You see, Bob played for the Clippers and later became a successful salesman. He eventually went on to become a radio personality, a successful lawyer and husband to one hell of a good-looking wife. Recently, he was named one the most important people in Vegas history. Believe it or not, he was even a co-creator of the Muppets. How could our little Petey compete?
    Alias, Bob could not live with out his beloved Pete. He mobilized all his resources, calling in every favor anyone ever owed him. He negotiated the use of the body of a handsome German man, on the condition that he still use part of his real name. The German was reprogrammed with all the spirit and imagination of Peter Martin. When Pete was littering the internet with his pipe dreams, he unknowingly left behind a bit of himself. Sure, those stories weren't who Pete really was, but within each string of words the essence of Pete lived on. His spiritual DNA was contained in each syllable. I am happy to say the operation was a success. Peter Martin is back, new and improved. As it turns out, his new face doesn't remind him of the old. He doesn't see that man that once was, he sees a man who now is. May I introduce you to Dr. (because he still has the paper work) Hans-Peter Martin.
    Well, maybe our little Hans hasn't changed all that much.Yep... Our old Pete came back! Lazy as ever.
    But lets face it folks, there is only one really cool Martin!
