    Sun 09.11Tampa BayL 13-24

    09.11.2005 - the lucky fan's explanation

    ok... let the worm4 curse begin. clearly i was not the lucky fan today. and my entire existance may be responbsible for the vikings ineptitude. my very existance may explain why the vikings haven't been to a super bowl since i was born, why their best season (1998) came when i was living in wisconsin and that i was in minneapolis to witness the loss to atlanta, that i tanked a job interview two days before 44-donut (assuring that our fates would forever be connected), why my two seasons as a season ticket owner resulted in the worst two regular season records since the les steckle era (5-11 in 2001 and 6-10 in 2002), and, of course, today.

    sure, you could make the case that it was daunte and burleson's faults for committing those first two turnovers (even i could see from my excellent and free seats that burleson ran the wrong route on both plays, and that daunte couldn't adjust), or that it was the playcalling by... who the hell is calling plays anyways? when it's 3rd and 1 and you've got kleinsasser in the backfield blocking for x-factor, why get cute and fake the handoff? if sending those two guys up the middle can't get you at least one yard against even the best defense, then we might as well shut it down now.

    and kicking the field goal from the 4 yard line with 6 minutes to go when down by 7 was just wrong, despite what dubay and morris say. if you go for the touchdown and don't get it, your defense has to stop the other team, but they'd have to do this if you made the field goal too. wouldn't it be better and easier for your defense to try to stop them at the 4 than the 30, assuming that the touchdown attempt failed? until the end, the defense was stopping the run pretty well. tampa's points (until the 4th) were only the result of brian "benchwarmer" williams and ralph brown getting picked on. so if tampa had first and 10 at the 4 with a 7 point lead and 6 minutes to play, they're gonna run 3 times and punt - meaning that the vikings would have had the ball at midfield needing a touchdown, instead of their own 20 and needing a touchdown, which was where they were after making the fieldgoal.

    incidently, i am cursed... the vikings lost because of me... i won't be going to cincinnatti next week... but i did get the free jersey (#1, "lucky charm" on the back) and the free fred smoot autographed football. and i'll get to chat with pa and dubay thursday morning at 9:30. tune in.

    actually, you know what, i take it all back. michael bennett bumped into me in the tunnel before the game. his bad luck rubbed off on me. it's all his fault.

    09.15.2005 - 15 minutes of infamy

    I swear that I was supposed to be a guest on the PA and Dubay show on KFAN this morning… but the fu^#ers never called, so I guess I was snubbed. Some contest this turned out to be: tickets to a terrible game, an undercooked bratwurst, no free road trips, a jersey that I would be too embarrassed to wear in public, and a signed football that my wife won't let me sell on ebay. This radio spot was supposed to be the redeeming moment; my chance to let the unemployed sport/bad funk enthusiasts of greater Minnesota know my feelings about what is shaping up to be another dreadful season of Vikings football… and maybe plug the website.

    Since I didn't get on air, here is how the interview would have gone:

    PA: Welcome back rubes and rubettes. We've got a special guest on line 1, last week's winner of the Lucky Charms Minnesota Vikings Lucky Fan of the Game contest, from St. Michael, Mr. Lucky, Jason Edgar. Whaddup Jay ya zaaaaaay!

    Me: Umm… is that a question? Well anyway, it's good to be here Paul.

    PA: I'd prefer if you called me PA, it sounds cooler.

    Me: That ship sailed when they put your picture on the website.

    Dubay: So lucky fan, what happened on Sunday? I'd hardly say that you brought any luck to the team. In fact it looked like your luck was more bad luck than good luck. Why would you bring so much bad luck to the game?

    Me: Has anyone ever suggested that you invest in a thesaurus? I digress - as it turns out there is really no such thing as luck. Only the random yet statistically inevitable occurrences that make up our existence. That and piss-poor coaching. This team just wasn't prepared to play against a legitimate defense. They completely underestimated the ability of a deep threat to draw safeties and linebackers out of the box; they overestimated the ability of their runningbacks to make plays when the offensive line wasn't giving them any room; and they were slow in making adjustments, as evidenced by the fact that Brian Williams was left in the game until after he had given up two touchdowns and it was clear that the Bucs were focusing their passing attack on him. Indeed, the only thing that may be considered a "lucky charm" that I could have brought to the game that would have benefited the Vikings efforts was a baseball bat which I would have applied to Mr. Williams' knee before kickoff.

    Dubay: I don't why you think you have the right to comment on players and coaches like that – you've never even played the game. Why, just this morning I was talking to Mike Morris….

    Me: Morris was a freakin' long snapper and you're 5'3" and 250. My God, look at yourself, you're covered with donut crumbs right now. Get back in your hole and keep your mouth shut.

    Dubay: Uhh… mmm… I got nothing.

    Me: Precisely my puffy friend. Your brain has become so addled with rube-initiated discourse involving small hands and pencils in ears that you've completely lost the ability to objectively study any event involving a local sport team and to offer any type of knowledgeable analysis. Ultimately, however, any comfort or loathing that can be injected into your listening audience is rendered irrelevant by the fact that it was just one game, hardly an adequate sample size to infer any meaningful conclusions. Was the Vikings offense as inept as it appeared? Or was the Buccaneer defense simply overwhelming? After 9 or 10 games, there should be sufficient data to draw a conclusion.

    PA: So… what's your favorite Hall and Oates song?

    Me: I once heard "Rich Girl" on the radio and didn't change the station, so I guess that'll be my answer. I'm pretty sure, though, that "Method of Love" is run on a continuous loop in hell.

    PA: OK, any thoughts on the upcoming game?

    Me: Just that one team will win, one team will lose, and your entire world will depend on it.

    Dubay: Oh yeah? Ever heard of a tie, lamo?

    Me: No one likes you.

    PA: Well, that was outstanding. In honor the Vikings' next victim, lets go to break while grooving to the funk stylings of Bomber and the Boys with their 1975 classic "Cincinnati Breakdown."

    Me: You just made that up, didn't you?
