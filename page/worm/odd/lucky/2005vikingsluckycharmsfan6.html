    Taken from vikings.com:

    The Lucky Charm

    NOVEMBER 23, 2005

    During the 2005 season, the Vikings have teamed with Lucky Charms in an attempt to find the luckiest Viking Fan. When selected, the Official Lucky Charm gets to attend the Vikings next game, home or away. As long as the team continues to win, the Lucky Charm continues to attend games. If you're feeling lucky, tell us in 50 words or less why you should be the Lucky Charm Fan of the Game.

    On October 30, the Vikings fell to the Carolina Panthers, dropping their record to 2-5. With starting Quarterback Daunte Culpepper sidelined for the season, the Vikings uncovered their "Lucky Charm." With this newfound four-leaf clover leading the way, the Vikings have won three straight games, including dramatic road victories at New York and Green Bay. You may think we are referring to new starting Quarterback Brad Johnson. But the true "pot of gold" has been worm 2, a software developer from Eagan.

    Selected on October 31 as the Vikings 6th Official Lucky Charm, worm 2 has helped put the team back in the thick of the NFC North Division race. "Maybe some of it is because I'm Irish" says Eric, who turned 31 years old on the day of his first game as the Lucky Charm (the Vikings 27-14 victory over Detroit on November 6).

    Eric has had a blast traveling to see the Vikings play on road. "The road trips have been great - seeing the different stadiums and just seeing the team compete and play well has been fun." His favorite moment of the winning streak thus far has been Paul Edinger's game winning field goal on Monday night at Lambeau Field.

    The Official Lucky Charm also receives an authentic jersey and a signed football. "I haven't washed the jersey yet," says worm 2. "My wife thinks that's really gross." worm 2 has his wife worm 9 and one and a half year old son to thank for this incredible experience. It was the poem submitted by worm 9 and her son that was selected as the winning entry. "Logan will definitely be getting lots of Vikings stuff for Christmas this year."

    Speaking of Christmas, Eric is ready to make the journey to Baltimore on December 25 if the Vikings can stay on a roll.

    Taken from startribune.com:

    DECEMBER 7, 2005

    It's been a good run for worm 2.

    It started as a promotion gone amiss. The Vikings, along with General Mills and KFAN, have sponsored a Lucky Charms Fan of the Game this season.

    A fan is chosen, gets tickets to a game and a jersey, and if the Vikings win, he or she gets tickets and accommodations for the next game -- home or away. After seven games, the Vikings had already gone through five contestants.

    "We were worried we were going to blow our whole budget on authentic jerseys," said a laughing Nathan Fix, who works in marketing partnerships for the Vikings.

    But it all changed with worm 2. The software developer from Eagan was chosen as lucky fan No. 6 after his wife, worm 9, completed the required 50-word essay on his behalf, in the voice of their 18-month-old son. As in, "Get my dad out of here, so I don't have to hear him yell at the TV," worm 9 said.

    His first game was the home contest against Detroit on Nov. 6, also worm 2's 31st birthday.

    "I figured maybe I'd get a trip to New York out of it," worm 2 said, alluding to the Vikings' game the next week against the Giants.

    Little did worm 2 know he would truly become a lucky charm, having been in attendance now for five consecutive victories (and counting). He's taken five different people with him on an odyssey that included a trip to Lambeau Field for "Monday Night Football."

    While he's quick to note that the turnaround is "more a tribute to the defense," worm 2 also recognizes there have been some fortunate outcomes during this streak. Three touchdowns by the defense and special teams to beat the Giants? A last-second victory at Green Bay?

    "Well, I am part Irish," worm 2 said.
