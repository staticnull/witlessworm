    2004 viking of the year by worm 4

    Before we decide on the 2004-05 (hopefully '05) Viking of the Year, a few thoughts on the upcoming season:

    It is already clear that the Vikings are going to the Superbowl. Who's their competition? Philadelphia? If any other team lost two Pro-Bowl cornerbacks and gained a wide receiver, no one in their right mind would expect them to improve - anyone still picking Philadelphia is just overloaded with liberal guilt after that whole Rush Limbaugh thing last year Washington? Just because Vermeil got lucky with Kurt Warner for one year doesn't mean that old coaches are the next great thing. Atlanta? Rookie head coach trying to install a new offense, fragile young quarterback, average running game, suspect defense - this team isn't even as good as the Randall Cunningham Eagles of the late 80's/early 90's, and they never went to a Superbowl. Seattle? Using the same logic as Charles Barkley when he refers to Jason Kidd as "Ason" (he's got no J[ump shot]), I like to refer to the Seattle Seahawks as the Seattle Seahawks because they've got no D[efense]. Green Bay? I don't know... does Favre have enough relatives who are on their death beds to make it through an entire season?

    (Wouldn't this be a great movie? Orange snowsuit-clad Packer fan travels to the bayou and picks off a Farve relative every week while sending threats to Bret that the ones who really matter (wife and child) will live only if the Pack goes to the Superbowl. Think Celtic Pride but directed by Tarantino, starring Adrian Brody plus 30 pounds as a cross between a non-cannibalistic Jeffry Dahmer and ... well, anyone from Door County, and John Goodman minus 30 pounds as the new sheriff who tries to cut through all the federal red tape and find the killer. You can add a side story about Goodman's character being a former lineman for Oklahoma or some other college football team and missing a block that lead to the quarterback getting paralyzed - and show how Goodman has struggled with nightmares because of it. This would serve as a nice parallel to the feelings of frustrations and helplessness that Goodman feels from his inability to save the Favres. Title? Howabout Second Down or Zone Blitz - z's are very hip nowadays. Wilford Brimley (with the accent from Hard Target) and Olympia Dukakis would be featured as Favre relatives; Omar Epps and one of the Wayans as consoling teammates... the possibities really are endless. But I digress...)

    The Vikings have a potentially dominant quarterback (legs, arm, int's and fumbles down last year), league best running game, best wide receiver in the league, top 3 offensive line, and a defense that improved to at least average. This is the exact same recipe for the 15-1 team of 1998.

    That said, the Vikings will go 12-4, win the division, and lose in the NFC championship game on a very suspicious series of plays. Suspicious in a Lakers-Kings game 6 kind of way. I'm just saying...

    Until then, it's time to select the Viking of the Year. Like a crown of thorns, the title is not a burden to be taken lightly; it is one that is worn by both the best and worst of men... but not in a sacreligous way. Past winners were:

    2003 - Kelly "Purple Haze" Campbell. Immediately after his designation as Viking of the Year, he disappeared. Gone were the 35 yard catches that seemed so easy. Oh, and the first drug offense goes unreported by the league. I'm just saying...

    2002 - Moe "X-Factor" Williams. Still pounding out a living as the Vikings 3rd down back. But let's face it, he's been the most consistent member of the offense for the past two years. And his hurry to get to the bathroom before Dave Dixon stunk up the place earned him a gift touchdown from Moss during the Denver game last year that will live on forever thanks to NFL Films.

    2001 - Byron "Pro-Bowl" Chamberlin. When not trying to earn a roster spot, Byron speaks to NFL rookies about the dangers of investing money in a local Krispy Kreme franchise.

    2000 - Johnny "Franchise" McWilliams. Whereabouts unknown.

    And that leads us to the 2004 nominees:

    1) Michael Bennett, nickname: Duck & Cover (or D.C.). Let's run down the fact sheet: Wisconsin native, Badger alum, uncle played for the Packers, has remarked on several occasions that he'd like to play for the Packers, has had some of his best games in Lambeau Field, is injury prone, is a free agent after next season, and he has two young running backs backing him up. As every speech/writing teacher/professor i've ever had has emphasized on the first day - know your audience. Is it any wonder that he was shot at while leaving a bar in St. Paul?... not Minneapolis, anyone can get shot at while leaving a bar in Minneapolis... St. Paul. Add to this the fact that he is a speed back playing on new turf that will slow him down and you have a recipe for disaster. If there was any way to send him to Miami for Ogunleye, and the Vikings didn't make the move, then we don't deserve to win the Superbowl. A little advice for D.C. - this is a make or break year for your career. Keep your mouth shut, invest in kevlar, and here's hoping that Ahman Green goes down with an\ injury tomorrow and Mike McKenzie is still available. I'm just saying...

    2) Tyrone Carter, nickname: Deuce. While a student at the University of Minnesota, Tyrone must have majored in photography, because he must have some really nice incriminating pictures of Ted Cottrell to have landed this job. The Vikings drafted Tyrone in the late rounds a few years ago for two reasons: 1) to make Sid happy, and 2) he could actually tackle people... couldn't cover receivers, but he could tackle. The ability to tackle people is great for a linebacker, but when your the third safety and the fourth option for nickle back, you don't see a lot of action on running plays. The Vikings figured out a few years ago that Tyrone would never be better than a special teams contributor and then promptly cut him and gave the job to Jack Brewer - another U of M alum (Sid!), another back-up safety, another good to very good special teams player, but cheaper. Seems like a good move. But now, Jack has been released and Tyrone brought back for a second try - hence the nickname Deuce. Does this make sense to anyone? It must make sense to Cottrell; he was with the Jets last year when Tyrone was brought in as a starting safety. Ted was fired, Tyrone cut, Ted hired, Tyrone signed. It doesn't take Joe Friday to figure this out.

    3) Marcus Robinson, nickname: Holy Ghost. If Robinson can be as effective for the Vikings as he was against the Vikings, well... we can hope. But then again, doesn't Chicago always have at least one average receiver who consistantly torches the Vikings? And doesn't this say as much about the Vikings' secondary as it does about the Bears' receivers? Injuries have hampered him for the past several seasons, but he came on late while in Baltimore and put up decent numbers. If he can stay healthy and be more productive than D'wayne Bates, the comparisons to Jesus may reach the level of "inappropriately numerous." We can hope... seriously, I like this guy.

    On a side note, is anyone worried about this offense having too many weapons? You figure that there are 4 receivers who warrant time on the field, 1.5 pass cathcing tight ends, 3.5 running backs that need carries, and a quarterback who needs to take the ball up the middle of the field a few times a game to stay productive. In order for every one of these people to live up to their potential, the Vikings will need to score about 60 points a game. I'm not complaining, I'm just saying...

    4) Mike Rosenthal, nickname: Bust. Webster's dictionary defines the word bust in several ways: a woman's busom; to smash or break; a flop or failure; a peice of sculpture representing a person's head, shoulders, and upper chest; etc. It is these last two examples that most aptly apply to Rosenthal. On the one hand, he was clearly the weakest link to the offensive line in 2003, this coming after his signing as a highly regarded free agent. The fact that the Vikings nearly drafted a right tackle with their first round pick and have already begun singing the praises of the new right tackle drafted in a later round should send up warning flags. The other definition though, a peice of sculpture, also applies, as Rosenthal has already been enshrined in the hall of fame... the Jewish Sports Hall of Fame. To paraphrase Woody Allen, who stole from Groucho Marx, I wouldn't want to belong to any hall of fame that would have someone like Jay Fiedler as a member. Side note, didn't Rosenthal go to Notre Dame? Is he still getting royalty checks from the producers of School Ties?

    Another side note, did Onterrio Smith and Nate Burleson ever get that whole S.O.D. thing resolved last year? Or has it been building up all year so that the two will end up in a night club in early october pimp slapping one another while stunned posse members and hoochies look on, contemplating gun play? Doesn't this seem like the kind of thing that winning teams just don't have to deal with and the Vikings have to deal with all the time?

    As before, if i am forgetting an obvious choice, please let me know as soon as possible. And remeber those comic K's. Winner to be announced before the season opener. You can tell me who you think sould win, but i'll probably just pick my favorite and lie to the rest of you with some made up vote count. Just like Florida, 2000.

    Cheers, The Shooter!
